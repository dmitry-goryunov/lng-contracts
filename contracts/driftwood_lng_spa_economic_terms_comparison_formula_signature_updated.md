## Slide 3: RED items: visible economic differences

RED items: visible economic differences

These are the items to model separately rather than treating the SPAs as equivalent.

Driver

Visible difference

Pricing formula

Vitol: multiplier over JKM+TTF; Gunvor: separately weighted JKM/TTF; Shell: pure JKM / pure TTF.

TTF FX conversion

Gunvor uses ECB reference rate; Vitol and Shell SPA2 use Bloomberg/Reuters 16:00 London.

ACQ sizing note

ACQ size is a contract-scale input, not a RAG-scored term; ratio-based items are scored versus ACQ.

Delivery shape

Shell has explicit winter-weighted seasonal tranches; Vitol/Gunvor are even/ratable.

Extension optionality

Shell SPA1/2 include two buyer-elected 5-year extensions; Vitol/Gunvor do not.

Credit support

Vitol/Gunvor use named guarantor/LC mechanics; Shell has more generic acceptable support.

Multiple-SPA coordination

Shell-only clause coordinates the two SPAs while preserving separate liabilities and caps.

Modelling implication: these are not just drafting differences. They change index exposure, optionality, hedge construction, settlement basis and credit valuation adjustment. ACQ remains a model input, not a RED term by itself.
